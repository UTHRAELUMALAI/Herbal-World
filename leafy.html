Leaves

Tulsi (Holy Basil)

Sacred in Indian culture, Tulsi is known for its potent healing properties.

Tulsi enhances immunity, reduces inflammation, supports respiratory health, lowers stress levels, and purifies the blood. It is also known for regulating blood sugar and cholesterol levels and offers relief from cold and flu symptoms.

Mint

Mint is aromatic and cooling, widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Mint aids digestion, alleviates nausea and headache, clears nasal congestion, improves oral hygiene, and has antibacterial and calming effects on the nervous system.

Neem

Neem is a powerful antibacterial and antifungal herb used in Ayurvedic medicine.

Neem purifies the blood, heals skin conditions, controls diabetes, boosts liver health, fights parasites, reduces inflammation, and acts as a natural insect repellent.

Curry Leaves

Used in Indian cuisine for their aroma and health-boosting nutrients.

They enhance digestion, reduce hair fall, control blood sugar, and improve iron absorption. Curry leaves are packed with vitamins A, B, C, and E and have strong antioxidant properties.

Banana Leaf

Traditional and eco-friendly, used as a food wrapper and plate.

Banana leaves contain polyphenols, promote digestion, prevent food contamination, and are environmentally sustainable. They enhance the flavor and aroma of food when steamed or served hot.

Sweet Basil

Basil is widely used in Mediterranean dishes and herbal medicine.

Basil supports cardiovascular health, improves digestion, reduces oxidative stress, and provides antibacterial benefits. It's also effective in reducing anxiety and mental fatigue.

Betel Leaf

Traditionally used in rituals and as a mouth freshener in South Asia.

Betel leaves improve oral hygiene, stimulate digestion, reduce cough and cold symptoms, heal wounds, and are believed to have aphrodisiac properties. They also help control blood pressure.

Moringa (Drumstick Leaves)

Moringa is known as a superfood due to its dense nutrient content.

Moringa is rich in calcium, iron, potassium, and protein. It boosts immunity, improves skin health, balances hormones, reduces cholesterol, and fights free radicals.

Guava Leaves

Guava leaves are a hidden treasure in natural remedies.

They help in treating diarrhea, lower blood sugar, reduce acne and dark spots, relieve menstrual pain, and promote healthy hair growth. Guava leaves also have antimicrobial properties.

Aloe Vera Leaf

Aloe vera is a succulent known for its soothing and hydrating gel.

Aloe vera hydrates skin, heals burns, treats constipation, lowers blood sugar, and boosts collagen production. It's used in skincare, haircare, and internal detox treatments.

Henna (Mehndi)

Henna, also known as Mehndi, is a traditional plant known for its natural dye and medicinal properties.

Henna leaves are widely used as a natural dye for hair and skin. They have antimicrobial, antifungal, and anti-inflammatory properties. Henna cools the body, soothes burns, treats dandruff, strengthens hair, and reduces scalp irritation. In traditional medicine, it is also used to heal wounds and control excessive sweating. When applied to the feet, henna helps in reducing body heat and improves sleep. The antioxidants in henna also support skin health and help relieve joint pain when applied externally.
