Types of Seeds and Their Benefits

  • Chia Seeds

    Chia seeds are small but mighty when it comes to health. They absorb many times their weight in water, making them excellent for keeping the body hydrated. Rich in fiber, they support digestive health, regulate blood sugar levels, and provide long-lasting energy. They also contain omega-3 fatty acids which contribute to heart and brain health, making them a great daily addition to smoothies, yogurts, or cereals.

  • Flax Seeds

    Flax seeds are a nutritional powerhouse filled with alpha-linolenic acid, a type of omega-3 fat known to improve heart health. They also contain high levels of lignans, antioxidants that may reduce cancer risk. Their soluble fiber helps in maintaining cholesterol levels and improving skin glow. They also supply selenium, copper, and folate, all of which aid in cellular repair and immune function. These seeds make a healthy snack when roasted and lightly salted or added to salads and granola bars.

  • Pumpkin Seeds

    Pumpkin seeds, also known as pepitas, are rich in zinc, magnesium, and healthy fats. They help boost immunity, improve sleep quality due to their tryptophan content, and support prostate health. Their crunch makes them a perfect addition to trail mix, soups, or roasted with a sprinkle of spice for a tasty snack.

  • Mustard Seeds

    Mustard seeds are widely used in Indian cooking, especially for tempering. Apart from their strong flavor, they offer excellent health benefits. They stimulate digestion, boost metabolism, and have anti-bacterial properties. Rich in selenium and magnesium, they help relieve inflammation and provide relief from aches and respiratory disorders.
